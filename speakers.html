There is a question you have answered several times a day, at least since your teens.

Nathan Eror

Twitter: @neror
When: Tuesday, June 12 @ 13.00
Slides

Talk: The Hustle
Synopsis: Many developers dream of working for themselves one day. However, when that day finally arrives, most developers find themselves ill prepared for reality. The moment you strike out on your own, you cease being a solely a developer or a programmer. You are now an indie, an entrepreneur, a freelancer, a contractor. While your programming skills might be the reason the business exists, "programmer" is only one of many jobs in your new business. No matter where your business falls in the spectrum between freelancer and product company, someone has to handle marketing, sales, business development, bookkeeping, accounting, and legal. Congratulations! These jobs are now all yours. Welcome to the hustle.

If you are interested in taking on all of these new roles (or you already have), you are not alone. In this session I'll share some of the principles, strategies, and tools that help me survive the daily hustle and have helped make it the most rewarding experience of my career.
Company: Free Time Studios

Nathan Eror

As the founder and lead developer at Free Time Studios, Nathan spends his days coding, teaching and writing, and he wouldn’t have it any other way. Since founding Free Time Studios in 2009, he has helped many organizations such as NASA, Thompson Reuters, and the U.S. Army ship their iOS apps and train their development teams. Nathan believes that a thriving community is the foundation of a great platform, and he devotes much of his time to the Cocoa community. His seminars and technical presentations are popular attractions at iOS and Mac developer conferences around the globe, and he is an active member of the developer community in Houston.

Matt Vaznaian

Twitter: @mvaznaian
When: Thursday, June 14 @ 13.00

Talk: Building a Backend for your iOS Application in Minutes
Synopsis:
When developing your iOS application, focusing on the primary features and overall user experience is where you most likely want to spend all your time. You know Xcode like the back of your hand and can build Storyboards in your sleep. Wouldn't it be nice to stick to what you know and create your backend using your frontend?

StackMob built its open source iOS SDK to make it easy to create your database on the fly and interact with all the server-side backend services. You can now create a database table in just two lines of Objective-C code. It enables you to save and fetch data or call your custom server side API in just minutes. We'll explore the implementation of StackMob’s open source iOS library and how it allows developers to integrate a robust backend into their iOS apps. You’ll leave the talk knowing how to build a backend service in minutes, not weeks.
Company: StackMob
